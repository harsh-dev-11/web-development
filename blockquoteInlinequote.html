The “Illusion of Competence” is real. This phenomenon was extensively documented by David Dunning and Justin Kruger in their famous 1999 study “Unskilled and Unaware of It.” They found that people with low ability in a domain often overestimate their competence because they lack the metacognitive skills to recognize their incompetence. This became known as the Dunning-Kruger effect.
