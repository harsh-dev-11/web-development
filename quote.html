HTML Quote Examples

1. Block Quotation without citation

<blockquote>
  The only way to do great work is to love what you do.
</blockquote>

Result:

The only way to do great work is to love what you do.
<blockquote>
  In the middle of difficulty lies opportunity.
</blockquote>

Result:

In the middle of difficulty lies opportunity.
<blockquote>
  Success is not final, failure is not fatal: it is the courage to continue that counts.
</blockquote>

Result:

Success is not final, failure is not fatal: it is the courage to continue that counts.

2. Block Quotation with citation (Article or a book)

<blockquote cite="https://example.com/article">
  The future belongs to those who believe in the beauty of their dreams.
  <footer><cite>Eleanor Roosevelt, "You Learn by Living" (1960)</cite></footer>
</blockquote>

Result:

The future belongs to those who believe in the beauty of their dreams.
Eleanor Roosevelt, "You Learn by Living" (1960)
<blockquote cite="https://example.com/book">
  It is during our darkest moments that we must focus to see the light.
  <footer><cite>Aristotle, "Nicomachean Ethics"</cite></footer>
</blockquote>

Result:

It is during our darkest moments that we must focus to see the light.
Aristotle, "Nicomachean Ethics"
<blockquote cite="https://example.com/research">
  The greatest glory in living lies not in never falling, but in rising every time we fall.
  <footer><cite>Nelson Mandela, "Long Walk to Freedom" (1994)</cite></footer>
</blockquote>

Result:

The greatest glory in living lies not in never falling, but in rising every time we fall.
Nelson Mandela, "Long Walk to Freedom" (1994)

3. Inline quotation without citation

<p>As they say, <q>practice makes perfect</q> when learning new skills.</p>

Result:

As they say, practice makes perfect when learning new skills.

<p>Remember the old saying <q>better late than never</q> applies here.</p>

Result:

Remember the old saying better late than never applies here.

<p>The phrase <q>time is money</q> has never been more relevant in today's world.</p>

Result:

The phrase time is money has never been more relevant in today's world.

<p>She always tells her students <q>knowledge is power</q> before every exam.</p>

Result:

She always tells her students knowledge is power before every exam.

4. Inline quotation with cite attribute and cite element

<p>Einstein once said <q cite="https://example.com/einstein-quotes">Imagination is more important than knowledge</q> in his famous <cite>Princeton lecture series</cite>.</p>

Result:

Einstein once said Imagination is more important than knowledge in his famous Princeton lecture series.

<p>According to <cite>The Art of War</cite>, Sun Tzu wrote <q cite="https://example.com/art-of-war">Know yourself and know your enemy</q>.</p>

Result:

According to The Art of War, Sun Tzu wrote Know yourself and know your enemy.

<p>Shakespeare's <cite>Hamlet</cite> contains the famous line <q cite="https://example.com/hamlet">To be or not to be, that is the question</q>.</p>

Result:

Shakespeare's Hamlet contains the famous line To be or not to be, that is the question.

<p>In <cite>Steve Jobs' Stanford Speech</cite>, he famously said <q cite="https://example.com/jobs-speech">Stay hungry, stay foolish</q>.</p>

Result:

In Steve Jobs' Stanford Speech, he famously said Stay hungry, stay foolish.

Key Points:

  • <blockquote>: Used for longer quotes that should be displayed as separate blocks
  • <q>: Used for shorter inline quotes within sentences
  • cite attribute: Provides the URL source of the quote (not visible to users)
  • <cite> element: Used to reference the title of works, authors, or sources (visible to users)
  • <footer>: Often used within blockquotes to contain citation information
